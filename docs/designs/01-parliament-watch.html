Parliament Watch

First Meeting of the Fourth Term 2026 — 24 February to 20 March 2026
Parliament finally addressed the teacher shortage crisis in Tamil schools. Three MPs raised infrastructure funding — but only one made a concrete commitment. The silence from Perak representatives was deafening.

Teacher Shortage

YB Sivakumar (Batu Gajah) raised the chronic shortage of Tamil-qualified teachers, citing 47 unfilled vacancies in Perak alone. The Deputy Education Minister acknowledged the gap but offered no timeline for resolution.

School boards: Write to your PPD requesting updated staffing numbers for your school and district.

Parents: Ask your school's PIBG chair about current teacher vacancies and report back.

NGOs: Submit a memo to the Education Ministry citing the parliamentary record (Hansard ref: DR-14032026-12).

Community: Share this brief with your school WhatsApp group to build awareness.

Infrastructure Funding

YB Kulasegaran (Ipoh Barat) secured a commitment for RM2M towards Tamil school repairs in Perak. However, YB Ramasamy noted that similar promises made in 2024 remain unfulfilled across Penang schools.

School boards: Check if your school was named in the RM2M allocation list — contact PPD Ipoh for details.

Parents: If your school building needs repair, document it with photos and submit to PIBG.

NGOs: Track the RM2M commitment — request quarterly progress updates from Perak state education office.

Community: If you know of unfulfilled 2024 repair promises, report them to us (reply to this email).

School Mergers

A backbencher's question revealed that 12 Tamil schools are currently under "enrolment review" — a precursor to merger discussions. No specific schools were named in the Hansard record.

School boards: If your enrolment is below 50, request a meeting with your PPD to understand your school's status.

Parents: Monitor — no action needed yet, but be aware that small schools may face merger proposals.

NGOs: File an official information request (SPI) for the list of 12 schools under review.

Community: If you hear of merger discussions in your area, tip us off (reply to this email).

MP Scorecard Update

YB Sivakumar (Batu Gajah) leads with 5 mentions this session — consistently the most vocal advocate. YB Kulasegaran (Ipoh Barat) was notably silent for the first time in 3 sessions. New entrant: YB Kesavan (Sungai Siput) raised Tamil school issues twice, both substantive.
