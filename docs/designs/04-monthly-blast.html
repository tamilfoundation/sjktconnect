Trend Lines
Parliamentary attention up
Mentions up 40% from January. The teacher shortage drove most of the increase, with three separate MPs raising it across different sittings.
Media sentiment down
Shifted from 70% positive in January to 61% in February. Closure threat stories dominated the negative coverage for the first time this year.
Estate school coverage new
First time estate schools appeared as a distinct theme in both Parliament and media simultaneously. The urban-rural divide is becoming the defining narrative.
Emerging Signals
  • Teacher vacancy data being cited by MPs for the first time — suggests our Parliament Watch briefs are being read in the House.
  • Two new parent advocacy groups formed in Johor, both focused on estate school preservation. Grassroots momentum building.
Fading From View
  • Transport issues — mentioned 8 times in December, zero in February. The Perak van pool solved it locally but the systemic issue remains unaddressed.
  • Digital infrastructure — dropped off entirely after Budget 2026 allocated nothing. The window may have closed for this cycle.
Opportunity Watch
  • YB Kulasegaran requested a meeting with Tamil Foundation — first direct engagement from the opposition bench this term.
  • Selangor state education committee accepting public submissions until March 15 — opportunity to present Tamil school data directly.
  • UNESCO Malaysia launching a "heritage languages in education" programme — Tamil schools could qualify for technical assistance.
School Spotlight: SJK(T) Ladang Sungai Tinggi, Selangor

Most mentioned school this month after winning a national STEM award with just 42 students. A reminder that small schools can punch well above their weight — and that enrolment numbers alone don't tell the whole story.
